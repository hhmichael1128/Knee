Knee flexion recovery (°)

Where you stand: 70° two weeks post-op → 135° now, against a right-side ceiling of 140–145°, so you're ~95% of the way back on bending range. The flat spot in May is the avulsion pause — note the curve resumed without losing ground. Range is no longer the limiter; quad strength is.

Function scores

LEFS — Lower Extremity Functional Scale: 20 questions about daily tasks (stairs, squatting, walking, sitting), scored 0–80, higher = better. A 9-point change is considered clinically meaningful. Yours: 21 → 51. Ryan's formal goal: 72/80 (= minimal limitation). Dashed green line on the chart.

KOOS JR — Knee injury & Osteoarthritis Outcome Score (short form): 7 questions on pain, symptoms and function, converted to 0–100, higher = better. Yours: 76.3 (Jul 8). Healthy young knees typically sit ~85–100; treat 85+ as your working target heading into sport testing. Dashed amber line.

IKDC — International Knee Documentation Committee subjective form: 0–100% knee function. Your only scores are pre-op (40–45%, i.e., a knee working at less than half capacity). Expect this to be re-measured around sport testing — ≥90% is the commonly cited return-to-sport benchmark.
